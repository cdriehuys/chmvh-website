Dr. Driehuys

Dr. Sunita Driehuys has been a practicing small animal veterinarian since 1994. She received her bachelorâ€™s degree from Franklin & Marshall College in Lancaster, PA. She received her veterinary degree in 1994 from the University of Pennsylvania with magna cum laude honors. She furthered her training with a one year internship that emphasized emergency and critical care medicine.

Since moving to the Triangle in 1996 she has practiced at several high-caliber small animal practices. These experiences led to her desire to create a veterinary practice that could combine the highest quality veterinary care with personalized attention and relationships.

This vision is now being fulfilled through Chapel Hill Mobile Veterinary Housecalls. A core practice philosophy is that the continuous pursuit of knowledge and education is the best way to care for her clients and patients. Dr. Driehuys has been certified as a Diplomate by the American Board of Veterinary Practitioners (ABVP) specializing in canine and feline practice. She lives in Chapel Hill, NC, with her husband and 3 sons.

Kari Ysland

Kari Ysland joined CHMVH in 2011. Kari grew up in Norway, but moved back to the States to continue her studies at the University of North Carolina at Wilmington. There she obtained her Bachelor's degree in Biology. Kari volunteered at the North Carolina Aquarium at Fort Fisher for two years before she was hired on as an aquarist technician. She was responsible for several exhibits of reptiles, amphibians and fish during her time there. She also took part in introducing operant conditioning to all the American alligators within the facility. She now enjoys living in a small town setting again and working at CHMVH.

In her spare time, Kari enjoys any form of art work, wildlife and gardening. Her two dogs, Jack and Viper along with her cats, Matrix, Amazon and Pardus, run her household and are also loving the country life.
